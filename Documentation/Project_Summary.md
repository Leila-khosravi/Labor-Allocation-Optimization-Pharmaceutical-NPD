# Project Summary  
## Labor Allocation Optimization for Pharmaceutical New Product Development

## 1. Project Background

The pharmaceutical industry operates in a highly competitive and complex environment where effective resource management is essential for successful new product development.

New product development requires collaboration among multidisciplinary teams with different expertise levels, skills, and resource requirements. Efficient allocation of specialized labor is critical for reducing development time, controlling costs, and improving productivity.

This project was developed at Biosun Pharmed Company to analyze labor allocation challenges and develop an optimization-based decision support model for pharmaceutical new product development processes.

---

## 2. Business Challenge

Pharmaceutical product development involves multiple activities that require appropriate allocation of skilled employees.

Key challenges addressed in this project include:

- Inefficient allocation of specialized labor resources
- Limited availability of skilled personnel
- Increasing development costs
- Long product development cycles
- Need for improving productivity and resource utilization

**Business Question:**

How can pharmaceutical organizations optimally allocate labor resources among new product development activities to improve productivity while minimizing cost and process completion time?

---

## 3. Project Objective

The objective of this project was to develop a decision-support model for improving labor allocation decisions in pharmaceutical new product development.

The project aimed to:

- Improve utilization of available human resources
- Increase team productivity
- Reduce development process costs
- Reduce process completion time
- Support effective resource planning decisions

---

## 4. Methodology

### Process Analysis

The project included:

- Analysis of new product development activities and workflow requirements
- Identification of labor allocation challenges
- Evaluation of employee skills and resource requirements
- Development of a structured approach for resource planning

### Mathematical Optimization Model

A multi-objective Mixed Integer Linear Programming (MILP) model was developed to optimize labor allocation decisions.

The model considered:

- Employee skill levels
- Training requirements
- Labor availability
- Development activities
- Cost constraints
- Time limitations

The optimization objectives included:

- Minimizing development costs
- Reducing process completion time
- Controlling labor-related expenses
- Maximizing productivity

---

## 5. Solution Approach

Two optimization approaches were applied:

### Enhanced Epsilon Constraint Method

- Used to solve the multi-objective optimization model
- Implemented using GAMS software

### NSGA-II Metaheuristic Algorithm

- Applied to solve complex optimization scenarios
- Used to address computational challenges of the optimization problem

---

## 6. Results and Business Insights

The proposed model was evaluated using nine sample problems.

Key findings:

- Increasing employee skill levels improved overall productivity.
- Increasing training hours reduced new product development time and enhanced efficiency.
- The NSGA-II approach achieved approximately 10% lower process costs compared with the exact solution approach.

The developed model provided a structured framework to support labor planning decisions and improve operational performance in pharmaceutical product development.
