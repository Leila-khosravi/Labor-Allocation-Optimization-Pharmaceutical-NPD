# Labor Allocation Optimization for Pharmaceutical New Product Development

## Project Overview

A large-scale business process optimization and decision-support project in the pharmaceutical industry, focused on analyzing complex workflows, improving resource allocation, and developing data-driven solutions for operational efficiency.

The project was developed to address workforce planning and resource allocation challenges in pharmaceutical new product development processes.

---

## Business Problem

New product development in pharmaceutical companies involves multiple activities requiring different expertise levels, skills, and resource commitments.

Ineffective allocation of specialized labor resources can result in:

- Increased development costs
- Longer process completion times
- Reduced productivity
- Inefficient utilization of available skills

**Business Question:**

How can pharmaceutical organizations optimally allocate labor resources among new product development activities to improve productivity, reduce costs, and support better operational decisions?

---

## Project Objectives

The objectives of this project were to:

- Analyze labor requirements within new product development processes
- Identify opportunities for improving resource allocation
- Develop an optimization model to support labor planning decisions
- Improve productivity while balancing cost and time constraints

## Methodology

### Process Analysis

The project included:

- Analysis of new product development activities and workflow requirements
- Identification of labor allocation challenges
- Evaluation of skill requirements and resource utilization opportunities
- Development of a structured decision-making approach for resource planning

### Optimization Model Development

A multi-objective Mixed Integer Linear Programming (MILP) model was developed to optimize labor allocation decisions.

The model considered:

- Employee skill levels
- Training requirements
- Labor availability
- Development activities
- Cost constraints
- Process time requirements

The optimization objectives included:

- Minimizing development process costs
- Reducing process completion time
- Controlling labor-related expenses
- Maximizing productivity

---

## Solution Approach

Due to the complexity of the optimization problem, two solution approaches were applied:

### Enhanced Epsilon Constraint Method

- Used to solve the multi-objective optimization model
- Implemented using GAMS software

### NSGA-II Metaheuristic Algorithm

- Applied for solving complex optimization scenarios
- Used to improve solution efficiency for computationally challenging problems

---

## Results & Business Insights

The proposed model was evaluated using nine sample problems.

Key findings:

- Increasing employee skill levels improved overall productivity.
- Increasing training hours reduced new product development time and improved efficiency.
- The NSGA-II approach achieved approximately 10% lower process costs compared with the exact solution approach.

The model provided a structured decision-support framework for improving labor planning and operational performance in pharmaceutical product development.
